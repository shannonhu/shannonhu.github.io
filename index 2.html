In 2015, My friend and I heard about this interesting art installation being shown in Shanghai, called “Rain Room”, and we decided to take a look. This “Rain Room” was said to have visitors walk through a downpour without getting wet.
At that time, for me, art was something that’s placed in distance between the viewers. Thus, when I was standing in the downpour, interacting with the rain, I was truly amazed.
The idea of creating an interactive experience, allowing viewers to be in the art and feel it, was especially inspiring.
Walking in the rain, watching the rain that surrounded me, reaching my arms out to stop the rain, my every movement made my relationship with Rain Room different than others. That experience changed my thoughts on art, and held me interested in interactive art.
(Rain Room mentioned here is an experiential artwork by Hannes Koch and Florian Ortkrass)
